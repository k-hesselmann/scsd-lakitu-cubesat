IMU MPU-6050 — I2C, 1 Hz polling
Parameter Unit Type Reliability Limitation FSW Use
Acceleration X, Y, Z m/s² or g Direct
High
 Reads 1g at rest (not 0). Cannot distinguish steady ascent from stationary. Detect launch jerk, burst event. Total vector magnitude for dynamic events.
Total acceleration magnitude m/s² or g Derived 1
High
 Still reads ~1g at rest and during smooth flight. Orientation-independent event detection. Standby→Launch, Cruise→Descent burst.
Angular rate X, Y, Z deg/s Direct
High
 Near zero at rest and during smooth flight. Not useful for altitude phases. Detect chaotic rotation during Launch or post-burst Descent.
Velocity (integrated) m/s Derived 1
Low
 Drift accumulates rapidly. Only reliable over a few seconds. Useless over full flight. Do not use for phase detection. Short-window cross check only.
Position (double integrated) m Derived 2
Very Low
 Drift makes this completely unreliable beyond a few seconds. Do not use. Not used in FSW phase detection.
Temperature °C Direct
Medium
 Not precise enough for scientific measurement. Monitors chip temperature only. Electronics health monitoring. Flag if dangerously cold at altitude.
GPS u-blox MAX-M10S — UART, 1 Hz polling, Airborne mode above 18 km
Parameter Unit Type Reliability Limitation FSW Use
Altitude (WGS84) m Direct
Medium
 Vertical accuracy 2x worse than horizontal (~5–15 m error). Unreliable if no fix. Altitude thresholds for Launch→Ascent, Ascent→Cruise. Cross check with barometer.
Vertical velocity m/s Direct
High
 Requires valid fix. Cold start delay at power-on. COCOM above 18 km if not in Airborne mode. Primary velocity source. Confirming condition for multiple transitions.
Horizontal velocity m/s Direct
High
 Requires valid fix. Component of total 3D speed for Landing detection.
Total 3D speed m/s Derived 1
High
 Requires valid fix. Primary condition for Descent→Landing. Catches horizontal drift as well as vertical.
Fix status flag Direct
High
 Must always be checked before trusting any other GPS value. Gate condition — if fix invalid, all GPS parameters ignored, fall back to barometer.
GPS-derived acceleration m/s² Derived 1
Medium
 Noisier than IMU direct. Differentiation amplifies GPS velocity noise. Cross check against IMU acceleration to detect sensor fault.
Timestamp UTC Direct
High
 Only available with valid fix. Accurate mission timestamp for all logged data.
Barometer MS5607 — I2C/SPI, 1 Hz polling, temperature-compensated
Parameter Unit Type Reliability Limitation FSW Use
Pressure hPa Direct
High
 Affected by wind gusts near ground. Requires temperature compensation from chip coefficients. Raw input for all barometer-derived parameters. Not used directly in phase logic.
Altitude (relative) m Derived 1
High
 Near ground, noise equivalent to 1–2 m. Baseline must be recorded at power-on in Standby. Primary altitude source for most transitions. Works across full 0–30 km range.
Vertical velocity (derived) m/s Derived 2
Medium
 Noisier than GPS direct velocity due to differentiation amplifying pressure noise. Confirming velocity source when GPS fix unavailable. Cross check against GPS vertical velocity.
Vertical acceleration (derived) m/s² Derived 2
Low
 Two differentiation steps from pressure. Very noisy. Not reliable for sharp events. Do not use as primary. Rough cross check only over long windows.
Temperature °C Direct
Medium
 Measures ambient air temperature, not electronics temperature directly. Ambient temperature logging. Required internally for pressure compensation calculation.

All parameters subject to median filter (window = 5 samples) before use in phase transition logic.  ·  GPS parameters only valid when fix status = 1.  ·  Barometric altitude is relative to ground-level baseline recorded at power-on.  ·  IMU integrated parameters (velocity, position) excluded from phase detection logic due to drift.
